#### Sandbox Island Game:
The game starts on the beach of a random uncharted island.
The island is procedurally generated and can be changed via the player.
The initial goal of the game is survival, as are most sandbox games like this.
You must complete a few goals including: finding fresh water, a source of food, a shelter, etc
The player can choose between several different scenarios in place of difficulties, these scenarios do not change game play, it only changes starting equipment.
- **Easy** - You are dropped off here by the  Wilderness Channel chalk full of survival tools, provisions, and a map. (Full health and hunger/thirst)
	- Starting time - 9:00 AM,
- **Normal** - You're plane back from your trip has crashed and all your plane mates have died. You start with a few essentials. (Half health, full hunger/thirst)
	- Starting time - 1:00 PM,
- **Hard** - You were sailing on the open waves with a small crew when suddenly a large typhoon came crashing your boat into the water. You awaken on the shore with not much more than the clothes on your back and a small pocket knife. (Half health, half hunger/thirst)
	- Starting time - 6:00 PM,
- **Extreme** - You have been kidnapped by pirates while on a cruise! After the pirates have taken all of your belongings, minus a few rags, they leave you on an island to die. Lying unconscious, near death, a sudden will to life emerges. (25% health, 25% hunger/thirst)
	- Starting time - 4:00 AM,

Initial thoughts:
- Features - Dynamic terrain, changeable by the player.
- Mobs, strange creatures inhabit the island, some herbivore plump pig-like quadrupeds, others, sleek wolf/lizard like predators, among other mobs.
- Fresh water vs salt water rivers/lakes - depending on the size of the island, there might be several bodies of water that are drinkable or undrinkable
- Caves and caverns - indents in sides of mountains can provide good shelters, but if you cant see the end, more might be waiting for you than you might think.
- Player stats - The player has to keep an eye on his/her health, hunger, thirst, and stamina bar when surviving.
- Indigenous creatures - intelligent life that makes a quaint home on the island. Approach them with caution as they may not take kindly to outsiders, but once you've gained their trust, the may provide a great asset for you when surviving.
- Multi player - not to be initially available, but a separate set of scenarios where you are dropped off with 2 or more survivors and you can choose to survive together, or separate and fight for arbitrary resources and/ or territories "Lord of the Flies" style.

#### Dungeons and Dragons DM Tool (and Campaigns):
A Super Mario Maker / Stronghold Map Editor like tool for creating fulling customizable tabletop RPG levels & campaigns. Would be built in Unreal Engine to utilize the realistic shaders.

- Editing:
	- Top down level editor with access to various models (walls, pillars, doors, floors, traps, statues, etc), materials (cobblestone, clean wood, old wood, grass, dirt, mud, stone brick, etc), and entities (monsters, NPCs, bosses, treasure, traps, etc) and other utilities (lights, triggers, spawn points, etc) that can be placed on a snap grid (toggle-able) in order to create virtual set pieces with multiple levels, floors and areas.
	- Can include features such as fog of war, point light based darkness FOW, creature editor (manually set a creature with preset actions and abilities), optional enemy AI (to allow for more automated experiences), customizable events and event triggers (when a door is opened trigger an event [a light turns on and 2 goblins spawn]), pop up text and notes (with readable text), etc
	- Can also include a Spore-like model creator where DMs can create custom buildings or set pieces (with scalable models and drag and drop materials) and save them as custom assets to place in their level or share on an in-game marketplace/ explore tab where players can share their own custom creations from within the DM tool

- Playing:
	- One DM would have a top-down view of the level, could pan and zoom around and would not have fog of war (though could see where the FOW is for the players/ can see which creatures are visible or not). The DM could control and manipulate the environment, enemies and events as well as special effects like lights, fire, wind, etc.
	- DM would have access to a menu that allows them to switch levels or manually spawn enemies, etc
	- One to four (or six or eight) players would have the option of a first-person, third-person or limited top-down view of the level (could be set and locked by the DM, like DM can set players to a third-person view and can set it so that players can’t switch views). Players would have the ability to move, attack with, or interact with their player and their surroundings (like a super game piece). For example, a player can move their character up to their movement speed, can choose from a menu of interaction options (determined by their surroundings or inventory, like attack goblin • open door • pick up loot • eat food/ drink potion • attempt to disarm trap with Thieve’s tools • attempt to open chest with silver key • etc)
	- Players can have saved player characters (similar to Terraria or Valheim) that they can customize (like D&D character creation mixed with standard character creation (like appearance). For example, I can choose my race and my class (and that will automatically set some stat bonus’) and then I can roll some, or manually type in my skill stats and I can customize my character’s appearance from a menu that changes based on their race selection.

The DM tool can be used by me to create premade campaigns that players can purchase as DLC and play through as either DM run parties or auto-run parties (with automated/pre-build NPCs and dialogue/ quests, automated triggers and events and AI powered enemies and bosses). Once someone has purchased a pre-made campaign DLC, they would have access to it in their own DM tool (as a saved level) for reference and editing.

Assets would be organized into theme packs that players can freely choose from and use. The game could be released with some basic fantasy packs and future theme packs can be released either as free or paid DLC.

This game should definitely be mod-able where anyone can create/import their own theme packs or enemy models. Maybe they can even import archived uasset files (like models and materials) and create custom tools, etc.

There should also be an in-game menu for DMs to explore and share their own custom creations (including custom models, events, or even whole custom campaigns) similar to Happy Wheels

This could be made using 2D graphics akin to other io games, but could also (and I would very much like to) create it using 3D graphics, with Three.js.
