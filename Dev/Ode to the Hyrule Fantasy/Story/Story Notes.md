Broad story points:

Regions with dungeons should have some element of story revolving around the dungeon itself and connecting to the overarching narrative: Ganon invaded central Hyrule prompting Zelda fled into lesser Hyrule to hide the triforce. Ganon established a base in death mountain to pursue her. He blocked the way to Kakariko Village and destroyed the port town to prevent Zelda or Impa from escaping. Zelda successfully hid the triforce but was captured by Ganon. Before she was taken, she was granted foresight by the triforce and asked Impa to search for a courageous hero who could gather the triforce and confront Ganon. Impa spent years in hiding, searching across lesser Hyrule for a hero. All the while, Ganon oppressed and killed the inhabitants, who took to hiding where they could to avoid Ganon’s minions, leaving the region nothing more than a ruineous wasteland.

Hidden Cove:
- A hidden away cove that has more or less escaped the notice of Ganon and his minions. Several people are hiding in the area.
- No dungeons.
- Begins the main quest
- Should point you to various areas of interests throughout the region (potion seller to northern pass, the nun to the graveyard and the shop keep to the port town? 
- What is the story of the cove itself?
- Backdrop: how hidden is this cove? How close to a main road are its entrances? Was this area home to any industry, homes or buildings? If there is a built shop, who visited that shop? Were there any other buildings? How did the old man end up here?

Port Town
- A once bustling town of trade and tourism. Its port was once an easy way to access northern Hyrule, bypassing Death mountain. Now destroyed, its inhabitants hide away in the ruins of the town.
- Two dungeons: Moon Keep & Old Dungeon
- Starts with level 2?
- Should be self contained, but gesture to the beach and northern pass (like the light house and abandoned ship)
- Backdrop: When was the city initially destroyed, what’s the timeline, what was the defense like? How does the city connect to Kakariko, were they able to help? Was it an ambush? Where did the attack come from? Was the town destroyed after Zelda hid the triforce?

Central Lake
- A wide lake along the “main road” that connects Kakariko with Port town. Swarming with octoroks and zora. Used to be a tourist destination.
- Two dungeons: Eagle Rock & Snake Isle
- Has a meat shop and a secret life cave
- Backdrop: What is the big pull to this lake? Does a main road go along it? What are the two islands? Why were temples built on them? Do they serve a different purpose? There is are several shops along the south and west coasts, could they be old tourist spots? Maybe the outpost on the west coast served to protect travelers going between port town and Kakariko. What about the east coast? There are a couple of secrets, but it’s the path to the river? How much travel did the mountains get? There is a mountain outpost where the power bracket is. How did people get there?

Lost Woods
- A once peaceful grove that grew up to the Kakariko road. Now overgrown, the magic of the lost woods makes travel through the area difficult except for a select few who have learned its trails.
- Two dungeons: Cursed Spring & Manji Point

Kakariko Graveyard
- The graveyard of Kakariko village, now cut off due to Ganon’s magic. The chapel next to the cemetery is in rough shape, but remains a beacon of hope. The priest, by Impa’s request, hides with the sacred master sword, awaiting the hero.
- No dungeons.

Death Mountain Foothills
- Mountain pass up from the Kakariko Graveyard. Connects the cemetery to lower death mountain. 
- One dungeon: Shadow Temple

Northern Pass
- The treacherous path from Northern Hyrule through death mountain ends here, though the way is not known, it is the eastern most part of the mountain range.
- One dungeon: Dragon’s Roost
- Contains the peninsula, the lighthouse and potion sellers husband
